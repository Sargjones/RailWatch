How this works

Brockville RailFan titles like "CN Train 368 Eastbound June 15, 2026" contain train symbols. When the same symbol appears in a Bayview Junction video from Trackside Ontario earlier the same day, RailWatch can calculate transit time and average speed.

A consist averaging 65 km/h eastbound from Hamilton to Brockville (207km) would arrive in approximately 3 hours and 10 minutes. If that consist carried tank cars at Bayview, the destination is east of Brockville — Montreal, or interchange to a US carrier at the border.

This is how we answer where products of interest are going.

About RailWatch Canada

RailWatch Canada is a public infrastructure intelligence platform tracking dangerous goods movement on Canada's freight rail network. It is a property of Critical TO, the Toronto infrastructure intelligence publication.

The platform exists because of a transparency gap: under Canada's Transportation of Dangerous Goods Act, freight railroads are only required to notify provincial fusion centres of dangerous goods routing — not the communities through which those trains pass. A resident A community observer watching tank cars roll through a CN station has no legitimate path to know what products are being transported, where they are going, or who is receiving them.

RailWatch does not claim to solve that gap. It documents it, contextualizes it, and builds the community observation infrastructure that will become meaningful as transparency improves.

What we cannot show (yet)

Real-time car positions, cargo manifests, specific shipper/consignee information, and bill of lading data are held by CN and CPKC under commercial confidentiality. Access requires carrier partnership agreements. RailWatch is pursuing these through formal channels and Access to Information requests to Transport Canada.
